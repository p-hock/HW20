Early Origins

Ancient Africa & Sri Lanka

People of 1000 BC constructed early drums by stretching animal hides over wooden shells. They used them for communicating over large distances and for ceremonial purposes.

Cymbals Emerge

Cymbals began being produced in ancient Turkey in 1100 BC, adding a whole new element to percussion.

Birth of the Snare Drum

The snare drum is invented in 1300s Europe by placing metal coils on a double-headed drum. This advancement produced much brighter and more complex rhythm sections.

Military Cadences

A Necessary Tool for Warfare

Military drummers played an essential role for armies in battle for thousands of years.

Communication on the Battlefield

The drum cadences set marching paces, called out orders, and helped soldiers regulate the loading and reloading of their weapons.

Morale

Military drumlines helped raise the spirits of soldiers charging into battle.

Modern Drum Kits

The Pieces

They're composed of a snare drums, bass drum, tom-toms, crash & ride cymbals, and the hi-hat.

Conception

In the 1840s drummers began experimenting with pedals to enable them to use their feet and utilize more drums.

Formative Moments

In the early 20th century New Orleans, percussionists of the growing Jazz genre began placing multiple drums together in formations that resemble the kits we see today.

Elements of Playing

The Groove

A groove sets the basic time-feel and provides a rhythmic framework for a song. Regardless of genre, all grooves are better with more cowbell.

Fills

They're a departure from the rhythm pattern. They often consist of a flurry of strokes that fill the space between the end of one verse and the begging of another. Is often a rhythm on the tom-toms, ended on the crash cymbal.

Solos

A time for the drummer to show off their virtuosity, skill, and creativity. Jazz Drummer Buddy Rich, and rock drummer of The Who, Kieth Moon, are too of the most legendary soloists in Drum history.
